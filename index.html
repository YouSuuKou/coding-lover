Beginning

Everyone has a variety of hobbies and interests. Some love badminton and enjoy the thrill of smashing a powerful shot; some are passionate about cycling, experiencing speed and excitement on the road; others are devoted to science, eager to explore new knowledge and expand their horizons. As for me, I am deeply passionate about information technology, especially programming.

History

I was first introduced to programming on September 1, 2023, when I started learning Scratch. Later, in September 2024, during my first IT lab class, I encountered the C++ language. Since then, I've learned about crypto, safety, concurrent, shell, Linux, and eventually moved away from using namespace std. I also discovered GitHub, through conversations with AI, was introduced to LaTeX. My latest open-source project was a crypto software in Python: https://github.com/YouSuuKou/crypt

Journey

Throughout my learning journey, I've adhered to the saying from Linus Torvalds: Software is like ***. It's better when it's free. I often spend at least three hours in one sitting while coding. I truly enjoy the sense of control over how the code runs and the output it produces. Every time I commit and push my code to GitHub, I feel a great sense of accomplishment. While developing, my copyright awareness is getting more and more clear.

By the way, I brought a program named pc-test, it is my first C++ project that published on GitHub.

While others are following stars on internet, such as Zhou Jielun, I'm chasing the god in computer - Linus Torvalds. He published the greatest open-source project: Linux Core. Today, the server of software we are using like bilibili, baidu, games, WeChat and so on are all using Linux Operating System, because it is safe, and Linus Torvalds never get any money from users except donation. This video shows Linus Torvalds' office. So someone asks me why do I open source, I will show a photo of Linus Torvalds.
